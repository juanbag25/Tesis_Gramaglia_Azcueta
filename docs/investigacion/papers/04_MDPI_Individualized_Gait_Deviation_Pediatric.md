## Abstract

Markerless motion capture is increasingly used in pediatric neurorehabilitation, yet its ability to detect patient-specific gait abnormalities in small and heterogeneous cohorts remains unclear.

This study evaluated a **smartphone-based markerless workflow (OpenCap integrated with OpenSim)** as a clinical assessment tool to support individualized planning in the context of **robot-assisted gait rehabilitation (RAGT)** by characterizing individualized gait deviations in **four pediatric patients with neurological gait disorders**, referenced against normative data from **30 healthy individuals**.

**Métodos:**
- Sagittal hip, knee, and ankle kinematics extracted, normalized, and converted into gait-cycle-dependent Z-scores
- One-sample Statistical Parametric Mapping (SPM) for group-level analysis
- Z-score heatmaps, phase-wise Z-score analysis, and per-patient kinematic overlays for individualized profiling

**Resultados clave:**
- Group-level SPM comparisons: **no significant deviations** between patient-group means and normative trajectories (p ≥ 0.05)
- Individualized deviation profiling identified **distinct, clinically meaningful abnormalities in every patient**:
  - Excessive swing-phase hip and knee flexion
  - Mid-stance knee extension deficits
  - Reduced terminal-stance hip extension
  - Markedly diminished late-stance ankle plantarflexion and push-off
- Several deviations exceeded |2–5| SD from the normative dataset
- Individual patterns consistent with each patient's clinical presentation

**Conclusión:** Smartphone-based markerless motion capture enables sensitive, individualized gait assessment even when group-level statistics remain nonsignificant, supporting its use as an **exploratory, decision-support framework** rather than as an outcome measure of RAGT.

**Keywords:** robot-assisted gait training, markerless motion capture, OpenCap, OpenSim, pediatric neurological disorders, gait deviation profiling

## Relevancia para tesis
- Demuestra el valor del **análisis individualizado** vs estadísticas grupales en poblaciones heterogéneas (cohorts pequeñas)
- Metodología de Z-scores por fase del ciclo y heatmaps — útil para visualizar desviaciones por paciente
- Aplicación en pediatría neurológica (cerebral palsy, etc.)
- Justifica uso de OpenCap+OpenSim como herramienta de **decision-support clínico**
- Marco metodológico replicable para detectar anomalías por paciente en n pequeños
