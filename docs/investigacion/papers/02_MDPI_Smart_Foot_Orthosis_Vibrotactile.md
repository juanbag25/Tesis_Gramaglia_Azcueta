## Abstract

Smart assistive technologies such as sensor-based footwear and walking aids offer promising opportunities for gait rehabilitation through real-time feedback and patient-centered monitoring. While biofeedback applications show great potential, current research rarely explores integrated closed-loop systems with device- and modality-specific feedback.

In this work, we present a **modular sensor-based system combining a smart foot orthosis and an instrumented forearm crutch to deliver real-time vibrotactile biofeedback**. The system integrates:
- Plantar pressure sensing
- Motion sensing
- Vibrotactile feedback
- Wireless communication via smartphone application

We conducted a user study with eight participants to validate the system's feasibility for mobile gait detection and app usability, and to evaluate different vibrotactile feedback types across the orthosis and forearm crutch.

**Resultados clave:**
- Pattern-based vibrotactile feedback was rated as more useful and suitable for regular use than simple vibration alerts
- Participants reported clear perceptual differences between feedback delivered via the orthosis and the forearm crutch
- Indicates device-dependent feedback perception

**Conclusión:** The findings highlight the relevance of feedback strategy design beyond hardware implementation and inform the development of user-centered haptic biofeedback systems.

**Keywords:** wearable sensors, vibrotactile biofeedback, smart foot orthosis, gait rehabilitation, plantar pressure

## Relevancia para tesis
Sistema integrado (closed-loop) que combina:
- Plantilla/órtesis inteligente con sensores de presión
- Muleta instrumentada con IMU
- App móvil para visualización
- Feedback vibrotáctil multimodal

Útil como referencia para:
- Justificación de feedback basado en patrones vs alertas simples
- Diseño de sistemas multimodales (presión + IMU + smartphone)
- UX de feedback háptico dependiente del dispositivo
- Validación de usabilidad en pequeños estudios piloto
