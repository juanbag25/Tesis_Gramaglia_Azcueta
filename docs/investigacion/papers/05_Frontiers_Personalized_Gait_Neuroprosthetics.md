## Abstract

Personalization of gait neuroprosthetics is paramount to ensure their efficacy for users, who experience severe limitations in mobility without an assistive device. Our goal is to develop assistive devices that collaborate with and are tailored to their users, while allowing them to use as much of their existing capabilities as possible.

Currently, personalization of devices is challenging, and technological advances are required to achieve this goal. This paper presents an overview of challenges and research directions regarding:
1. **Interface with the peripheral nervous system**
2. **Interface with the central nervous system**
3. **Requirements of interface computing architectures**

**Recomendaciones clave:**
- The interface should be **modular and adaptable**, providing assistance where it is needed
- Novel data processing technology should allow for **real-time processing while accounting for signal variations** in the human
- **Personalized biomechanical models and simulation techniques** should be developed to predict assisted walking motions and user-device interactions
- The advantages of interfacing with both the brain and the spinal cord or the periphery should be further explored
- Technological advances should focus on **learning on the chip** for further personalization
- **Low energy consumption** is required for longer use
- **In-memory processing combined with resistive random access memory (RRAM)** is a promising technology for both personalization and energy efficiency

**Keywords:** Neuroprosthesis, Resistive random access memory, neural interface, personalized devices, perspective, Embedded Artificial Intelligence
